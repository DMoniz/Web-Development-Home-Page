Why Help?

As you can see from the graph the global water crisis is in a critical state. There are over 2 billion people who do not have access to clean drinking water. You might think that the increase in number of people having access to safe drinking water is a positive, however that is just because of the population increase. You can see that the people without access to safe drinking water has been increasing every year since 2010 as well. If we do not act this will continue to get worse and worse. We have a ways to go before fully completing this goal but everyone makes a difference!


Approximately a third of the world do not have access to improved sanitation facilities. This is a real problem as it creates many diseases such as diarrhea. 72% of these people live in Asia. The population of Asia is around 4.7 billion meaning more than half of Asia's citizens do not use improved sanitation facilities. Please direct your help to these places as it is really needed.

Over the last century, our use of freshwater has been increasing every year. This is mostly occurring due to population growth and the global increase of temperatures due to global warming. According to a NASA-led study, many world freshwater sources are being used faster than they can be replenished. Many people believe we will not have enough freshwater by as soon as 2040. To help fight this reduce your use of water (don't waste it) and donate to science investigating other sources of water (i.e. NASA).

Women and girls spend an estimated 200 million hours hauling water every day.
